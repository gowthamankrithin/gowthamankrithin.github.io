About Me

I am Krithin, a visiting student at the International Centre for Theoretical Sciences, Tata Institute of Fundamental Research. I am presently studying two dimensional vorticity in the context of geophysical fluid dynamics where I use numerical simulations that employ applied mathematical techniques to study turbulence in the ocean. I have a passionate interest in computational fluid dynamics and aerodynamics. Historical research in fluid dynamics has focused on explaining turbulence with the knowledge of laminar flow. I am intrigued by how, in nature, turbulence is the rule while laminar flow is an exception. With the pressing need for sustainable development, I see the road to the engineering marvels of the future is through taming the fascinating chaos turbulence is.
